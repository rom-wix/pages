Competitor Data Import / Export Capabilities

Migration friction analysis across 11 booking & practice-management platforms

Beauty Onboarding project · Updated June 5, 2026 · Framed around how easily a business can export its own data to migrate into Wix

Three export dimensions below: clients, bookings / calendar, and service catalog (the service menu and pricing). Client export is near-universal; bookings and especially the service catalog are where platforms diverge. "Client Secure" refers to SimplePractice (clientsecure.me is its booking domain).

Bookings / calendar export at a glance

  • ✓ Yes — self-serve  Acuity, Fresha, Jane, Jobber, FareHarbor
  • ◐ Partial  Vagaro (calendar .ics + report; full CSV via support), Mindbody (free reports; full export paid), SimplePractice (past only), HoneyBook (calendar sync + analytics only)
  • ✗ No self-serve  Square (Professional Services), Booksy (support request)

Service catalog export at a glance

  • ✓ Yes — self-serve  Vagaro, Fresha, Jobber
  • ◐ Partial  Acuity (embedded in appointment CSV / API), Mindbody (pricing-options report; full via paid export), Jane (via reports only), SimplePractice (via billing reports), FareHarbor (reports & API)
  • ✗ No  Square (item library exports, service list does not), HoneyBook, Booksy

Key takeaways for onboarding

  • Client export is table stakes — every platform offers it self-serve (Mindbody is the exception, gating a full export behind a fee).
  • Bookings/calendar export is the first divider — Acuity, Fresha, Jane, Jobber, and FareHarbor let you export the schedule yourself; the rest gate, limit, or charge for it.
  • Service-catalog export is the rarest — only Vagaro, Fresha, and Jobber export the service menu cleanly. Square, HoneyBook, and Booksy don't export services at all, so the menu must be rebuilt on import.
  • Fresha is the standout for beauty — the only beauty-relevant platform that exports clients, bookings, and the service catalog self-serve (Jobber matches it but is field-service oriented).
  • Hardest to leave: Square and Booksy lock both bookings and services behind support/specialist requests; Mindbody charges for the full export (~$500).
  • Payment cards are never portable — card-on-file data is PCI-protected and cannot be exported on any platform.
Sources are official platform help centers except where noted. The Mindbody ~$500 figure comes from third-party migration guides and should be confirmed against current Mindbody pricing before relying on it.
