Final validated result. Fade 1-hour stochastic extremes: short when raw %K(5) ≥ 95, with TP/SL = 1.5×ATR(14) and a 24-hour limit. Positive on every in-sample and holdout split of all three pairs — including AUDNZD, a pair it was never fitted to — and it survived independent replication, negative controls, and a parameter-plateau test. Combined holdout 2021–2026: ~3,850 trades, +3,044 pips net.

How the answer was found

Part 1 — AUDCAD M15 (50k bars)

None of 4,572 indicator cells beat the 2-pip cost in-sample. A lone survivor — CCI(14) ≤ −250 longs in the Asia session — looked good on the holdout (+2.3 pips, t≈2.9) but verification flagged regime drift and selection effects.

Part 2 — AUDCAD M5 and H1

M5: conclusively untradeable (cost ≈ 40% of any target). H1: the first timeframe with 73 parameter-stable rules, all short-side. The M15 survivor failed its pre-registered transfer test to both timeframes — correctly exposed as resolution-specific. A float32 boundary bug at round stochastic thresholds was caught and fixed.

Part 3 — AUDNZD and NZDCAD H1 (16 years each)

The H1 stochastic fade transferred verbatim: positive in all 6 pair×split cells, strongest on AUDNZD (t = 2.4 on both splits). The EMA-distance rule that verification had flagged failed on both new pairs, as predicted. Four verification agents: four passes.

The rule

SHORT when raw stochastic %K(5) ≥ 95 on H1
  take-profit = entry − 1.5 × ATR(14)
  stop-loss   = entry + 1.5 × ATR(14)
  time limit  = 24 bars (exit at close) · enter at next bar open
at real spreads, add the filter: 8-bar ROC ≥ 98th trailing-500 percentile (then TP = 1.0 × ATR)

Caveats that travel with the result

Per-pair t-stats are modest (0.9–2.4) and the three pairs share currencies, so they are not independent samples. At real spreads the plain rule is near breakeven on the weaker pairs — the ROC combo (≈4–6 pips gross per trade) is what survives realistic costs. The edge decays within 1–2 bars of delayed entry, so execution timing matters. 2026 year-to-date is flat-to-negative on all pairs. Mandatory next step before any live risk: forward / paper validation.

Methodology: triple-barrier labeling (entry at next open, ATR-scaled barriers, stop wins same-bar ties, timed exits at close), sequential non-overlapping trades, in-sample/holdout firewall with embargo, parameter-plateau test, multiple-testing deflation, adversarial verification (independent replication from raw data, delay/random/leak-probe controls, pre-registered transfer tests).
