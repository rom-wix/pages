Cross-platform patterns that repeat

What every serious adoption engine seems to ship
01

Persona gate → every downstream surface

HubSpot, Shopify, Webflow, Squarespace, Canva, Notion all branch the entire UX from one early question. Personalization isn't a module — it's a switch at the root of the tree.

02

Templates > blank canvas — always

Notion, Airtable, Monday, Asana, Shopify, Squarespace, Canva, Etsy: all lead with a template gallery. None lead with a tutorial. Starting from "partial answer" beats starting from zero.

03

Checklists pair with progress bars

Slack, Notion, Shopify, Intercom, HubSpot. Ordered steps, visible % done, dismissible-but-re-surfaceable. Still the highest-ROI activation pattern in SaaS.

04

"Try it" deep-links — not documentation

Linear, Figma, Atlassian, Canva, Shopify: announcements include a button that routes straight to the feature. Reading about a feature and trying it happen in one click.

05

Empty states are onboarding moments

HubSpot, Shopify, Notion. The "nothing here yet" screen is the one place every user lands — so it should be the most curated, educational, CTA-forward surface in the product.

06

Behavior-triggered >> time-triggered

Canva's 650 variant catalog, Etsy's milestone emails, Intercom's action triggers. "Did feature X by day 3" beats "Day 3 since signup" at nearly every metric — open, click, and adoption.

07

Admin-owned adoption dashboards

Salesforce Lightning Usage, Monday AI Governance, Figma DS Analytics. Big platforms hand adoption metrics to the admin, not the end user. Whoever can see the funnel owns it.

08

The product teaches the product

Slackbot, Notion's "/" command, Linear's ⌘K, Figma's multiplayer. The highest-ROI adoption isn't a tour — it's a feature that demonstrates itself by being used.

09

Category-specific learning hubs

HubSpot Academy, Salesforce Trailhead, Webflow University, Etsy Seller Handbook. In-product links to a deep learning resource that's branded, searchable, and often certified.
